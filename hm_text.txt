It Starts With One Slack Command.                           Copy:     Open Slack. Type /newjd. A short form appears. Eight fields: your email, role title, company, seniority, location, salary range, responsibilities, must-haves. Fill it like you are texting a teammate. That is the heaviest lift you will do all week. Within hours a polished JD draft lands in your DM, in your company voice, ready for edits.                      Build notes    :            Layout: centered MagicCard sized like a Slack modal. Title "New role", eight stacked fields, primary submit. AnimatedGridPattern behind at low opacity.            Components: ShineBorder on the frame. Fields auto-fill at roughly 18 chars/sec. Submit gains a BorderBeam on the final field; a Slack ping appears top-right after submit.            Animation: typing runs once on enter; after submit a fan of dots bursts outward (spring 220/18). Reduced motion: render form filled.            CTA: secondary link "See the eight fields".                           Scene 4. We Draft. You Stay The Editor.                           Copy:     The first draft is on us. Open it between meetings. Each candidate is one page. Tap Proceed, Hold, or Reject. The next one slides in. The whole batch takes 20 minutes. Submit, and recruitment sequences every Proceed for interviews the same day. No PDFs. No spreadsheets. No guessing.                      Build notes    :            Layout: centered phone frame in a MagicCard, three decision buttons beneath, "Submitted" toast lifts after the third tap.            Components: a one-pager scrolls itself through key sections; a finger cursor taps Proceed, the card slides off, the next slides in. ShineBorder on the active button. After three cycles, toast: "Decisions submitted. Recruitment is on it." Counter chip: "27 of 28 reviewed".            Animation: card slide is x translate over dur-base, ease-out; tap pulse scales 1.0/0.96/1.0 over 200ms. Reduced motion: hold third one-pager with toast up.            CTA: secondary link "Preview a sample shortlist link".                           Scene 7. Days, Not Weeks.
